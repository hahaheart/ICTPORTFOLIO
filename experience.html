ICT EXPERIENCE

When I first encountered coding in this subject, I thought it would be hard, I thought that it would be too complex for my understanding and that it would be very difficult for me to study the lessons. But as I tried to learn the basics, it slowly became very easy. In fact, it even became fun. When I first started coding, it felt like I was entering a completely new world. There were so many terms and concepts to grasp—variables, loops, conditionals—and it all seemed overwhelming at first. But as I spent more time with the code, it started to make sense. It was like learning a new language, and each piece I understood opened up a bit more of the landscape.

One of the most exciting parts of coding is problem-solving. There's nothing quite like the feeling of finally fixing a stubborn bug or finding a solution to a complex problem. It can be challenging, of course—sometimes I’d spend hours trying to track down a single error—but when I solved it, it was like hitting the jackpot. It made all the effort worth it. I also found that coding allowed me to be creative. Whether I was building a small script to automate a boring task or developing a more complex application, I could see my ideas come to life. I loved that I could create something from scratch and then watch it work, knowing I had built it with my own hands. That sense of accomplishment was incredibly rewarding. But coding wasn’t always easy. There were times when I felt frustrated, especially when I couldn’t figure out why something wasn’t working. It tested my patience, and there were moments when I felt like giving up. In those times, I learned the importance of persistence. I found that if I just kept at it, trying different approaches and asking for help when needed, I could usually find a solution. It was a lesson in resilience.

It can be hard to keep up, but I’ve come to enjoy the challenge. There's always something new to explore, and it keeps things interesting. In the end, coding has been a journey filled with creativity, problem-solving, collaboration, and continuous learning. It has its highs and lows, but I wouldn't trade it for anything. It’s given me the tools to create, innovate, and contribute to something bigger, and that’s a pretty amazing feeling.
